The VR in virtual pilot

by Nathan Barnes

With the HTC Vive and Oculus Rift you too can step into the cockpit of a Cessna 172 and assume the role of a weekend pilot without a fear of colliding into the side of a mountain.

As VR (Virtual Reality) technologies make headway in the market, users and game/simulator developers are looking at new ways to use digital technology to increase their immersion within a gaming environment with the possibilities to, as Matthew Schnipper says "Put on these goggles, go nowhere, and be transported anywhere".

Laminar Research, X-Plane 11 (2017) Cover

While VR is not a new concept, having its day in the late 80's and early 90's, modern hardware has observed a renewed vigor in VR possibilities. In particular, the usage for immersion and realism within flight simulators such as Laminar Research's X-Plane 11 and Lockheed Martin's Prepar3D, are not only considered a recreational flight sim, but as an educational, training system.

The key draw-card for flight simulators when used as training instruments for would-be pilots is to achieve realism, and gain a feel for flight, without the real-world costs of lessons or only having first hand flight experience to realise if flight is for them.

With an Oculus Rift at the ready, as described in a VR Flight World article, a depth of realism was achieved through VR within X-Plane 11 that allows physical head movement to look around the cockpit and experience flight, practice maneuvers, take-off and landings and to feel a part of the simulator as seen through the eyes of a real pilot.

The question remains if current VR technologies can evolve to the point where the simulated environment is on par with the real world, and be an extension to living reality. Only time will tell.
