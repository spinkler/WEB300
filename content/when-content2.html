Welcome to Augmented Reality (AR).

AR is rapidly growing in popularity because it brings elements of the virtual world, into our real world Reality Technologies

While Virtual Reality (VR) simulates real-life environments via a full headset, AR converges with reality by overlaying, and "placing" virtual objects and information into the real world that we can then interact with through devices such as our mobile phone or AR Glasses.

A Pikachu standing on the street in Pokémon GO

With the release of popular game titles such as Pokémon GO on mobile devices, millions of people have now experienced AR and the new dimension, and level of immersion offered to game play by overlaying virtual Pokémon into our real world environment.

However, AR goes much further than bringing already digital media to life, with text media such as Dungeons & Dragons converging with AR technology to overlay the fantasy world on top of your gaming table to add a new layer of immersion into the game setting.

What AR achieves is overlaying information seamlessly on top of our existing environment, allowing both the virtual and real world to converge. With the technology continuing development the question is: will we one day see everyone walking around donning a pair of AR glasses, or will technology develop to the point where glasses and mobile devices are not required to augment our reality, with individual implants becoming so passive to the point of full immersion and normality within our everyday lives?
