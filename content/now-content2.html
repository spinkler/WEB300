The Contemporary 'Gamer'

by Nathan Barnes

From the original Snake on Nokia 6110 mobile phones to Angry Birds and Candy Crush on today's smartphones, we have had access to some type of gaming media on the go for decades.

With the development of digital mobile technology from the black and white days of Snake to full-fledged gaming experiences with graphical quality of Playstation right in the palm of our hands, the definition of what a 'gamer' is has changed, along with the general gaming demographic.

The stereotypes of young, angry, pale and isolated gamers are wrong. Van Badham
King (2012)

As smart phones have become a part of everyday life, with an increase in mobile gaming, a dramatic shift in gaming demographics has seen more children, female, and older age groups reach for their mobile devices to indulge themselves in some pixel gaming goodness.

With mobile games such as Pokemon GO and Candy Crush having 752 million and 2.73 billion downloads respectively on mobile platforms, the definition of who a 'gamer' is, has surely too shifted.

While mainstream gaming development studios may cater for a predominately-male 18-25 demographic as described by GameSparks.com with the dismissive attitude of mobile gaming being "not real gaming" due to its casual nature, Pokemon Go and Candy Crush have proven that AAA games are not the only type of gaming media individuals have to consume in order to consider themselves too, a gamer.
